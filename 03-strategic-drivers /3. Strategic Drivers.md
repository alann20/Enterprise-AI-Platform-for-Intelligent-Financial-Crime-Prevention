# 🎯 3. Strategic Drivers

The **GlobalBank Enterprise AI Transformation Program** is guided by a set of strategic drivers that connect business priorities, technology modernization, governance requirements, and enterprise risk management. These drivers define why the organization must transform its current operating model and establish a secure, scalable, reusable, and governed Enterprise AI Platform.

Together, the strategic drivers provide direction for investment decisions, architecture roadmaps, platform capabilities, governance controls, and implementation priorities. They ensure that every AI initiative contributes to measurable business value while supporting regulatory compliance, operational resilience, customer trust, and sustainable global growth.

---

## 💼 4.1 Business Drivers

GlobalBank must improve its ability to prevent financial losses, protect customers, accelerate investigations, and make risk decisions in real time. The increasing volume and complexity of digital transactions require intelligent capabilities that can detect suspicious behavior, correlate activity across channels, and support employees with accurate and timely insights.

The business drivers also focus on improving operational efficiency, customer experience, employee productivity, and speed to market. By creating reusable enterprise AI capabilities, GlobalBank can reduce duplicated investments, accelerate the launch of new financial services, and support consistent growth across regions, business units, and digital ecosystems.

### Key Business Drivers

* Reduce fraud-related financial losses.
* Improve customer trust.
* Increase digital transaction security.
* Accelerate investigation processes.
* Reduce operational costs.
* Improve customer experience.
* Enable real-time decision-making.
* Increase employee productivity.
* Create reusable AI capabilities.
* Improve time to market.
* Support global business growth.

---

## ⚙️ 4.2 Technology Drivers

GlobalBank's existing technology landscape includes legacy fraud platforms, isolated Machine Learning models, fragmented data environments, and inconsistent integration patterns. These limitations reduce visibility, delay decision-making, increase maintenance costs, and make it difficult to scale AI capabilities across the enterprise.

The technology transformation focuses on establishing shared AI services, reusable APIs, real-time data processing, cloud-native platforms, and event-driven integration. A centralized Enterprise AI Platform will provide the scalability, resilience, interoperability, and standardization required to support multiple AI use cases across regions and business domains.

### Key Technology Drivers

* Modernize legacy fraud platforms.
* Replace isolated AI models with shared enterprise services.
* Centralize AI infrastructure.
* Standardize AI integration patterns.
* Enable real-time data processing.
* Establish an Enterprise AI Platform.
* Implement reusable AI APIs.
* Adopt cloud-native architecture.
* Enable event-driven integration.
* Improve platform resilience and scalability.

---

## 🛡️ 4.3 Governance Drivers

The adoption of Machine Learning, Generative AI, and Agentic AI introduces new risks related to explainability, bias, privacy, security, accountability, regulatory compliance, and automated decision-making. GlobalBank therefore requires a consistent governance framework that defines policies, decision rights, controls, responsibilities, and approval processes across the complete AI lifecycle.

Governance must also provide traceability of models, prompts, agents, data sources, decisions, and third-party AI services. By standardizing architecture, model validation, risk assessment, regulatory reporting, and Responsible AI controls, GlobalBank can scale innovation while maintaining human oversight, auditability, transparency, and institutional accountability.

### Key Governance Drivers

* Establish enterprise AI governance.
* Standardize model approval processes.
* Enable auditability and traceability.
* Implement Responsible AI controls.
* Manage model and agent risks.
* Improve regulatory reporting.
* Create consistent architecture standards.
* Govern third-party AI providers.
* Manage AI lifecycle risks.
